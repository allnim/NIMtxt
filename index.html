New Internet Media
fighting fake news
with provenance

Provenance refers to the history of digital content assets (image, video, audio recording, document). NIM enables consumers to make qualified decisions about the provenance of any news (short or long text). By preventing scammers and spammers from impersonating others NIM offers a simple way for the ecosystem to identify the source of news as well as the creator/provider of the news. In 2020, it was estimated that disinformation in the form of fake news costs around $78 billion annually. It’s not only costly, it’s also deeply disturbing that most people starts out to mistrust any online publishers and/or journalist unless their work can be easily referenced with established provenance. NIM will change that!
